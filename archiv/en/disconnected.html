My desire to stand out against social networks united with the need to give my creativity more space and freedom. That's why we meet in your e-mail inbox.

 

–


The number of followers and likes distorts our perceptions and opinions. If something changes our perception or opinion (inside), I assume that it also changes our behavior and subsequent creation (outside). When you add the power of the algorithms, I feel like a lot of the individuality gets pushed aside and the result is more of a compromise that tries to meet those expectations and demands.

 

–

 

The advantages of social networks could be (relative) accessibility and (potential) reach – without much effort, anyone can engage in contact with the whole world. Is it really so? More or less yes, but…

 

I myself spent a fair amount of time on Instagram creating and consuming content. However, I got to the point where I had enough. Out of more than a thousand accounts that I followed only a few dozen "favorites" were displayed to me. Their content gradually began to merge in my feed – they became winners of the algorithmic roulette who followed the rules of the platform, but often at the expense of their own authenticity. This curated content has been heavily diluted with paid content (i.e., the price we pay for accessibility). The benefits disappeared and the entertaining medium became a stereotypical time waster for me. So one day I disconnected.

 

At first glance, "disconnecting" presents various pitfalls – more effort (how much time does it take you to use social media?), uncertainty (what certainty does social media give us?), lower reach (what % of your followers saw your last post?) or maybe the popular FOMO (do we really need to know "everything"?). So is it worth it?


For me personally, the disconnection helped to open my eyes wide to the real world. I go to galleries more often and at the same time look for inspiration in everyday life. I travel and look around. I'm finally reading more. I get the most crucial ideas from conversations with friends. I have to make some effort to gain new information and experience. In the meantime, I am definitely missing a lot of information, but I certainly do not have a lack of new information – on the contrary I find that this information are much more often relevant and striking.
